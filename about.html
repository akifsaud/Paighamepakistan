Chairman's Message

A Message from the Chief Organizer

As the Chief Organizer of Paigham-e-Pakistan, I firmly believe that every child deserves the opportunity to learn, grow, and become a productive member of society — regardless of the circumstances of their parents.

Our initiative was born from the understanding that the children of convicted prisoners are among the most vulnerable members of our society. Without intervention, they face a high risk of falling into the same cycle of crime and marginalization.

Through education, we are not just providing knowledge — we are providing hope, dignity, and a path forward. Every scholarship we grant, every book we distribute, and every mentorship we offer is an investment in Pakistan's future.

Prof. Muhammad Meraj Siddiqui

Chief Organizer, Paigham-e-Pakistan

Our Purpose

Vision & Mission

Our Vision

To create a Pakistan where the cycle of crime is broken through education, where every child — regardless of their family's circumstances — has access to quality education, and where peace, human dignity, and compassion form the foundation of justice.

We envision a society that does not punish children for the actions of their parents, but instead empowers them with the tools to build a better future for themselves and their communities.

Our Mission

To provide comprehensive educational support — including scholarships, learning materials, mentorship, and career guidance — to the children of convicted prisoners across Sindh's jails.

To collaborate with government ministries, educational institutions, and civil society organizations to build a robust framework for prisoner rehabilitation, de-radicalization, and community reintegration through education.

Compassion

Treating every individual with dignity, empathy, and respect regardless of background.

Integrity

Maintaining the highest standards of transparency and accountability in all operations.

Innovation

Pioneering new approaches to social rehabilitation through education and community engagement.

Have Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Paigham-e-Pakistan and our programs.

Paigham-e-Pakistan is the world's first revolutionary human rights and peace initiative focused on breaking the cycle of crime through education. It provides comprehensive educational support and scholarships for children of convicted prisoners in Sindh's jails, in collaboration with multiple government ministries.

Children of convicted prisoners held in Sindh's jails are eligible. The program covers school-age children and provides support for tuition fees, textbooks, uniforms, and learning materials. The initiative aims to ensure that no child is denied education due to a parent's incarceration.

The initiative operates in collaboration with the Ministry of School Education and Literacy Department, Ministry of Prisons Sindh, and Ministry of Universities & Boards. This multi-ministry collaboration ensures comprehensive support across the education and justice sectors.

You can support Paigham-e-Pakistan by spreading awareness, partnering with us as an institution, volunteering your expertise in education or counseling, or contacting us to explore collaboration opportunities. Visit our Contact page for more details.

The program provides comprehensive educational support including full tuition coverage, textbooks and learning materials, school uniforms, personal mentorship, career counseling, family support services, and regular academic progress monitoring.
